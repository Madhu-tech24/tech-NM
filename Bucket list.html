3.Eiffel Tower,Paris,France

Not yet

The Eiffel Tower, a temporary structure built for the 1889 World's Fair, was almost demolished and was initially disliked by Parisians. It changes height with the seasons, is repainted every seven years to prevent rust, and houses a secret apartment at the top, belonging to Gustave Eiffel himself. The tower was the world's tallest building for over 40 years and still serves as a crucial radio transmission tower, a role that saved it from being dismantled

4.Sydney,Australia

visited

Sydney, Australia's oldest city, boasts the world's largest natural harbor and hosts spectacular New Year's Eve fireworks, making it a major global destination. The city is a melting pot of cultures, with over 250 languages spoken by its diverse population, and offers more than 100 beaches for recreation. Iconic landmarks include the Sydney Harbour Bridge, nicknamed "The Coathanger," and the architecturally famous Sydney Opera House

5.Mount Fuji,Japan

visited

Climb to see the sunrise

Mount Fuji is Japan's tallest and most iconic active volcano, famous for its perfect cone shape, which is actually three volcanoes stacked atop one another. It is a sacred and culturally significant mountain with thousands of annual visitors, featuring Shinto shrines and beautiful views of the nearby Fuji Five Lakes. Its last eruption was in 1707, and it remains a popular destination for hikers, artists, and spiritual pilgrims.
